The Roles Of Animals In The War

Animals were used lots in the first world war.  Horses were used for transport,  dogs were used for couriering and companionship.  Pigeons were used for carrying notes and other things.  These are some of the uses for animals in the 1st world war.  My task is to describe what animals did in ww1. So let’s get started.

What were the jobs for animals in ww1?

Even though there were hundreds of jobs and animals in ww1 I'm just going to list a few.  

Dogs

Dogs were incredibly useful in ww1. They were used as sentries, (dogs who are trained to alert soldiers of gas attacks and ambushes.)

couriers, (They pass reports and data, to big for birds to carry, to the rest of the soldiers or other places)

nurses, (these brave dogs ran through the battlefields giving medical supplies to injured soldiers.)

pest control(Because the soldiers lived in trenches rats lived everywhere, these dogs found, killed and retrieved rats to help soldiers) and pets.

Horses

Horses were also incredibly useful. They were mainly used for transport. By transport, I mean carrying supplies, wounded soldiers, pigeons and sometimes even weapons(there are pictures of horses with machine guns on their backs.

Pigeons

Pigeons are mainly used for conveying messages to different parts of the army and long-distances. Soldiers would tie messages to one of the pigeon’s legs and send them off.  For example, some pigeons were used to fly messages over the black sea to Europe.  When I say surveillance I think spies and gadgets, not pigeons but they worked.  In fact, in ww1 pigeons were one of the best forms of surveillance around(amongst human spies of course).  Soldiers would put little mini cameras on the front of them and send them off to survey enemy armies.  The cameras were self clicking so they would automatically take photos every once in a while.   Because they used film, when they got home they had to develop it.
